Introduction to Burhanpur

Welcome to Burhanpur, a historic city in Madhya Pradesh, India, situated on the north bank of the Tapti River. Founded in 1380 and named after Sufi saint Burhan-ud-Din, it has been a meeting point of many cultures under the Faruqi, Mughal, Maratha, and British rulers.

With a population of over 210,000 people, it's famous for its Mughal architecture, textile industry, and as the place where Mumtaz Mahal passed away, which inspired the creation of the Taj Mahal. Explore its rich heritage, bustling markets, and delicious cuisine.

Historical Value

Burhanpur's history is ancient and spans several centuries:

14th-15th Century

Founded by Malik Nasir Khan of the Faruqi Dynasty in 1380. It became the capital of the Khandesh Sultanate and a thriving trade center under rulers like Miran Adil Khan II.

Ancient Fort

15th century architecture

Mughal Era (1601 onwards)

Annexed by Emperor Akbar in 1601. Served as a key outpost; governors like Abdul Rahim Khan-i-Khanan built many gardens and water systems. Prince Parviz and Shah Jahan lived here. Mumtaz Mahal died in 1631 while giving birth, initially buried in Zainabad before being taken to Agra.

Mughal Architecture

17th century monuments

The Black Taj Mahal

Built by Abdul Rahim Khan-i-Khanan in memory of his wife, this black stone monument is often called the "Black Taj" due to its resemblance to the Taj Mahal. Though smaller in scale, it features similar architectural elements with intricate carvings and a serene garden setting. It stands as a testament to love and architectural prowess of the Mughal era in Burhanpur.

Black Taj Mahal

17th century monument

Maratha and British Period

Conquered by Marathas in the 1720s under Peshwa Bajirao and again in the 1750s. Handed over to the British in 1818. Active in the 1857 Uprising and freedom movements.

Importance of Burhanpur

Why Burhanpur is Important

  • Strategic Location: Gateway to Southern India, on trade routes between Agra and Surat.
  • Economic Hub: Largest power loom center in Madhya Pradesh; textile, cotton, and oil mills.
  • Cultural Diversity: Mix of Hindu, Muslim, Buddhist, and Jain influences; languages like Urdu, Hindi, Marathi.
  • Historical Link: Inspiration for Taj Mahal; featured in Jules Verne's "Around the World in 80 Days."
